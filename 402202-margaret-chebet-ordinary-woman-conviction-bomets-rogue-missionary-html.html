Margaret Chebet: Ordinary woman behind conviction of Bomet's rogue missionary

Publish date: 2024-05-01

- When news of Gregory Dow molesting children at his orphanage hit the headlines, Dow fled back to the US hoping to evade justice

- One relentless woman, Margaret Chebet, became detective and pursued the case until Dow was convicted by the authorities

- TUKO.co.ke had an interview with her as she reminisced the harrowing journey in search of justice for the voiceless victims

Margaret Chebet was scrolling through Facebook when she bumped into news that Gregory Dow, a supposed white missionary from Pennsylvania had been abusing young girls at a children's orphanage in her Kapkatet backyard in Bomet.

As she would learn, two teenage girls had escaped from the orphanage and shared horrifying stories of what Dow does behind closed doors.

Furthermore, a 9-month-old baby had died earlier from the same orphanage.

When the news hit the headlines, Dow closed the home and fled back to the US, hoping to avoid justice.

Chebet took it upon herself to play detective and bring the perpetrator to justice, as complicated as the case was given that it touched on two different countries.

TUKO.co.ke had an interview with Chebet, who reminisced the harrowing journey that recently saw Dow imprisoned for more than 15 years in addition to paying $16,000 (KSh 1.7 million) in restitution.

"Some people I spoke to were teachers at the home who admitted that they were not qualified but earned KSh 5,000 per month as salary to teach the many subjects at the same time," said Chebet.

Chebet decries the laxity in the Kenyan authorities as her initial efforts to have the case handled in Kenya were thwarted.

"I approached the police officers who were close to the facility and aired my grievances, adding that time was of the essence since Dow had already fled back to the US. They took me round in circles until my stay in Kenya elapsed," she remembered.

The highs and lows

She admits that the energy the story gave her was divine, especially given the coincidences between the orphanage being in her neighborhood in Kenya and Dow living ten minutes from her house in the US.

"Every time I wanted to give up on the case, a new door opened. Like this day I was just scrolling through a difference case on Facebook and landed on the ex-wife to Gregory Dow, who had shared about his history with molestation," she explained.

The one thing that gave Chebet the urge to pursue the case further was learning that any American citizen is capable of being charged in the US even if he commits a crime in a different country.

Since years had elapsed since allegations against Dow came out, Chebet faced the hurdle of convincing people that she had enough evidence against the rogue missionary.

"After being frustrated from all corners, I decided to just leak the information to our local media house here in Lancaster who published it. From then on the police took up the case and sent detectives to Kenya to gather more evidence," she underpinned.

Dow arrested and charged

In July 2019, Gregory Dow was arrested and Chebet was called to be the voice for the victims as the suspect pled guilty to four counts of abuse against young girls from as young as 11 years old.

Chebet is happy that the victims got justice, a verdict that she calls fair given the duration it had taken for it to be picked up.

"If God didn't make me follow up this story, Dow would still be on the streets prowling on the next victims. It is just sad that nothing will ever pacify the victims who suffered under his care, including those who lost their lives," she concluded.
