Political analyst Herman Manyora now says President Uhuru Kenyatta should postpone the forthcoming 2022 General Election in order to save the country which he said was on the verge of "collapsing".

In his latest edition broadcasted on his YouTube channel on Saturday, March 20, Manyora said, with the current state, the country was incapable to hold a general election unless raft measures are taken.

The political commentator listed, the poor state of the economy and the ravaging COVID-19 pandemic as the main factors to suspend the polls.

" We ought and in fact, we should postpone the 2022 elections. We must address these problems first before we can think of going to the next election, if we don't do it, this country will crash," said Manyora.

On the economy, Manyora said it was not viable to spend billions to hold a general election at a time when the economy was on its death bed.

The fiery analyst said the cost of living was high, the unemployment levels had escalated to greater heights and the public debt was also biting.

" The economy is not sustainable, how do you want to go into an election at the time when 50% of Kenyans were going to bed hungry?" posed Manyora.

COVID-19

The University of Nairobi lecturer further argued that the third wave of the COVID-19 pandemic might get worse and there is a need to suspend the polls to focus on the disease.

The professor said that politicians were superspreaders of the disease and President Uhuru Kenyatta's ban on political gatherings was not enough to curtail the disease.

"You want to be president in 2022 but the people might not be there, this disease is not sparing even leaders. We don't know how this third wave will go and there might be a fourth wave," said Manyora.

Stealing public funds

The don also linked the recently reported theft of public funds scandals to the 2022 General Election.

He said politicians were looking for money to finance their 2022 political campaigns.

"If we stop this election, stealing will go down and money that is being lost everyday could be used to improve the healthcare system," said Manyora.

On how the country could postpone an election, the analyst said a referendum can be held which can be voted for electronically to reach the decision.

He also said the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) could be implemented immediately where Uhuru continues to be the head of state and Raila Odinga the prime minister until a later date when the country will be ready to hold an election.

In a separate story, Manyora described Baringo senator Gideon Moi as "prince" who will swiftly clinch power in 2027 after the reign of whoever will be endorsed by Uhuru in 2022.
