Mike Sonko demolishes business premises in CBD after owner allegedly destroyed Buruburu school, chur

Publish date: 0001-01-01

- Sonko was irked by the businessman who allegedly sent goons to destroy a school and church in Buruburu

- The county boss visited the school and vowed to bring down the business premises of the investor located in CBD

- The Nairobi leader claimed the rogue businessman had put up a car wash without approval of the county government

Nairobi Governor Mike Sonko has made real his threats and brought down business premises of a man who allegedly sent armed goons to unlawfully demolish a school and church in Buruburu Phase Four on Saturday, May 25.

The county boss who rushed at the scene to stop the destruction vowed to equally bring down the establishment of the businessman who had allegedly sent the goons to destroy the school in order to grab the land.

County officials reportedly acting on the governor's directive on the morning of Monday, May 27, demolished a business complex that housed Pimp My Ride East Africa, formerly Simmers Restaurant, on Kenyatta Avenue in the Central Business District (CBD).

The governor was irked by the demolition incident in Buruburu which disrupted learning and threatened the security of learners and locals, prompting him to intervene and restore order.

Speaking at the school on Saturday, the furious county boss said he knew the investor and was operating a car wash within the CBD which had not been approved by City Hall.

"I know him, he has a car wash at (former) Simmers in the CBD and I want to assure you the way he has destroyed this facility is the same way his building will go down," warned the governor.

The county leader said it was wrong for anyone to threaten city residents by sending men armed with pangas, axes and other machinery to demolition a school and rough up women and children.

Traders who operate within the demolished business premises said there were surprised with the destruction without any notice from the county government or court.

James Saitoti, the manager of Pimp My Ride which was part of the complex, asked the governor and the owner of the promises to sort out their differences without interfering with their businesses.
