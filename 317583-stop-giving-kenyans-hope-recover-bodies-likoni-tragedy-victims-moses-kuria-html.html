Stop giving Kenyans hope you will recover bodies of Likoni tragedy victims - Moses Kuria

Publish date: 2024-02-11

- Kuria advised the government to instead shift its ficus on cleaning up the mess by firing Kenya Ferry Services (KFS) bosses

- The legislator claimed seven minutes was lost as Mariam Kigenda and Amanda Mutheu helplessly drowned

- Mariam and Amanda plunged to their death when a car they were in slipped off MV Harambee ferry at Likoni Channel

- Kuria joined several other MPs in calling for sacking of KFS bosses following the Likoni ferry accident

Gatundu South Member of Parliament Moses Kuria has termed as an exercise in futility the ongoing recovery mission in Likoni, Mombasa county, where a 35-year-old mother and her four year-old daughter drowned on September 29.

The vocal lawmaker ruled out the possibility of finding the bodies of the two victims who have been trapped in the deep Indian Ocean waters for a staggering eight days.

In a Facebook post on Saturday, October 5, Kuria stated the government was giving Kenyans false hope by promising to recover bodies of Mariam and Amanda, insinuating it could be too late to find the victims.

"Kenya Ferry Services should stop giving Kenyans hope that they will recover the bodies and the vehicle. A whole seven minutes was lost watching an innocent lady and her child die. The attention should shift to firing the CEO and ensuring this will never happen again," the MP advised.

Kuria joined several other politicians who had also demanded sacking of KFS bosses following the Likoni ferry tragedy which exposed the embarrassing state of the government's disaster preparedness.

In a joint statement issued on October 1, the lawmakers led by Likoni MP Mishi Mboko put on the spot KFS managing director Bakari Gowa and Navy Commander Major General Franklin Mghalu after their men failed to respond to the disaster despite being stationed a few metres from the scene of tragedy and also questioned the role of the Kenya Coast Guard.

“We recently passed a law that created the Kenya Coast Guard. The guard’s cardinal role is to provide safety in Kenya’s territorial waters. We want to know from the Coast Guard’s Director-General Brigadier Vincent Loonena where the Coast guards were and what they were doing,” the MPs' statement read.

The Kenyan government on Saturday indicated it will seek help from its South African counterpart to help retrieve bodies of Mariam and Amanda.

This was disclosed by Transport CS James Macharia who assured the government will do all it takes to retrieve bodies of the victims and the car they were in at the time of their drowning.

"All that needs to be done shall be done. We had an expert from Sweden and we are having others from South Africa who are coming in the next few days to join the rescue operation,” the CS said.
