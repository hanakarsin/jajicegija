Moi in trouble as journalist threatens to sue

Publish date: 2024-02-09

- A photojournalist has threatened to sue Baringo Senator Gedion Moi

- The journalist, Cheboite Kigen who works for the Nation Media Group claims former President Moi’s son has been using his photo without consent

- Kigen has accused Gideon Moi’s team of threatening his life over the matter

Retired President Daniel Arap Moi’s son Gideon Moi risks cooling his heels behind bars after a Nation Media Group photojournalist threatened to sue him for using one of his photos without consent.

According the photojournalist, Cheboite Kigen, the Baringo Senator has been using a photo he took with him back in 2014 and is now using it on his 2017 campaign posters without his permission.

Kigen said efforts to have a sit down with Moi to mull over the matter have been unsuccessful.

“Every time I attempt to meet him to discuss the matter, his personal assistant Peter Chelang'a blocks me and threatens to beat me up,” Kigen told a local newspaper in a candid interview.

The journalist added that Moi’s team recently barred him from covering any event for Baringo Senator.

Kigen also revealed that he has been receiving text messages from Moi’s PA who has been threatening his life.
