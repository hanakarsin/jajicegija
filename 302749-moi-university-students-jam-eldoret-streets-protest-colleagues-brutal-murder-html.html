Moi University students jam Eldoret streets in protest of colleague's brutal murder

Publish date: 2024-05-14

- The medical student was killed by her alleged boyfriend over suspected love gone wrong

- The assailant was also beaten up by boda boda operators before he was rescued by the police

- The man slit Ivy Wangechi's throat before hitting her on the head and trying to escape

Moi University students from the school of medicine have taken to the streets of Eldoret town to express their displeasure over the death of their colleague who was murdered by her lover in broad daylight.

Operations were brought to a standstill at the Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital as the students barricaded the roads leading to the facility before they marched to Eldoret Central Police Station.

The university Vice Chancellor Isaac Kosgei confirmed Ivy Wangechi died after she was rushed to the hospital following the fatal attack.

“It is a very sad day for us. This morning we lost one of us to an attack by an unknown assailant. We are working with the security forces to piece together what happened and we will update you in time. I want to request all of you to remain calm,” he said.

Eldoret West OCPD Zachariah Bitok later addressed the students saying they would investigate and ensure justice was served.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, the suspected was reported to have carried a newly sharpened axe and a knife in a white sack and waited for the deceased at the hospital gate.

The 26-year-old was undertaking her practicals at the facility and had just finished visiting the wards.

The man is reported to have pounced on the sixth year medical student, slit her throat before hitting her on the head and tried to escape.

Angry boda boda operators gave chase and caught up with him.

He was thoroughly beaten before he was rescued by police from the angry mob which was baying for his blood.

He is currently admitted at the hospital where he is receiving medical attention.
