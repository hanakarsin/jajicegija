Uhuru dismisses claims he was booked a KSh2.1M room in Rio

Publish date: 2024-08-22

- President Uhuru Kenyatta has been accused of being part of the Rio fiasco

- Uhuru is said to have been booked a KSh 2.1 million room in a luxurious cruise ship in Rio but never used it

- State House has now responded to the claims first made by Team Kenya's Chef de Mission Stephen arap Soi

President Uhuru Kenyatta has moved to dismiss claims that he was booked a room worth KSh 2.1 million in Brazil for the recently concluded Rio Olympics.

READ ALSO: Uhuru Kenyatta linked to Rio scandal

State House said that the president was never booked any room and that only the Foreign Affairs ministry is responsible for booking accommodation for the president whenever he travels out of the country.

The claims were made by Team Kenya's Chef de Mission Stephen arap Soi who said that the National Olympics Committee of Kenya (NOCK) had been instructed to book the room in a luxurious cruise ship for the president.

READ ALSO: Government takes action on NOCK after Olympic fiasco

According to Soi, Uhuru was likely to visit Brazil during the Olympic Games and therefore the room was booked for him in advance.

His sentiments come as police officers are carrying out investigations in the shambolic manner in which team Kenya to the Rio Olympics was handled and allegations of theft and misappropriation of funds.

READ ALSO: Keino's family trolled online after rumours allege they are wearing stolen Olympics gear

Already, the government has disbanded the NOCK and a number of officials including Soi arrested and released on cash bails.

Despite the mismanagement, Kenyan athletes put their best foot forward and won six gold, six silver and two bronze medals for the country.
