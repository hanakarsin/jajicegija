Kianjokoma: 6 Emotional Photos from Burial Ceremony of Slain Embu Brothers

Publish date: 2024-02-05
  • The requiem mass for the two brothers was held at the Kianjokoma Primary School grounds in Embu county
  • National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi, MPs Rigathi Gachagua (Mathira) and Moses Kuria (Gatundu South) were some of the notable leaders at the event
  • The two brothers were later laid to rest at their father’s farm in Kithangari Kianjokoma in Embu North

Emotions ran high during the burial ceremony of twin brothers Benson Njiru and Emmanuel Mutura at Kianjokoma, Embu county.

Hundreds of mourners turned up to pay their last respects to the two brothers in a funeral ceremony held at Kianjokoma Primary School.

The two were later laid to rest at their father’s farm in Kithangari Kianjokoma in Embu North.

Some mourners donned t-shirts with the message 'Justice for Benson and Emmanuel'.

Among the mourners was National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi who joined the rest in demanding justice for the duo.

''I once again expressed my deepest sympathies to the family following this tragic loss. As we continue to demand justice for the slain young people, I call upon all the security organs concerned to expeditiously conclude their investigations and bring all those culpable to book,'' said Muturi.

TUKO.co.ke sampled below some of the emotional photos from the burial ceremony:

1. Emotional mother breaks down

Catherine Wawira, the mother of the slain brothers, said that she lacks words to express how much pain the death of her sons had caused her.

2. Mother's love

Catherine Wawira, the mother of the slain Kianjikoma brothers, could not stand at the sight of the caskets bearing her sons' bodies.

3. Bodies of the two brothers arrive at Kianjokoma Primary School

The bodies of twin brothers Benson Njiru and Emmanuel Mutura arrived at the Kianjokoma Primary School from the mortuary in two separate hearses.

4. Bodies of the two brothers arrive at Kianjokoma Primary School grounds

On Friday, August 13, the two bodies were moved from the Embu Level Five Hospital mortuary to Kianjokoma Primary School grounds for the requiem mass.

5. Justin Muturi among the mourners

National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi joined the family of John Ndwiga and hundreds of mourners at Kianjokoma Primary school in Embu county during the requiem mass of brothers Benson Njiru and Emmanuel Mutura, who lost their lives at the hands of the police.

6. Political leaders among mourners

During the requiem mass for the two slain, National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi led a host of politicians, including MPs Rigathi Gachagua (Mathira) and Moses Kuria (Gatundu South) Kianjokoma brothers.

Interior Cabinet Secretary (CS) Fred Matiang’i assured the family of two brothers who were found dead after being arrested that police officers connected to the killing will face the full force of the law if found guilty.

The CS met with the family of Njiru, 22, and Mutura, 19, on Thursday, August 12 and assured them that the investigations would be completed on time.

"Two investigative agencies including Internal Affairs Unit and Independent Policing Oversight Authority are on it, and I promised the father that we will do everything we can to get justice," the CS said.

Matiang’i said he met Ndwiga in Nairobi in Inspector-General of Police Hillary Mutyambai and Attorney General Paul Kihara.

"I have been briefed that the investigations are going on well and will give us their findings soon. We will follow the law and direction of the probe,” he added.

The two bodies were found at the Embu Level Five Hospital mortuary three days after being arrested for allegedly disobeying the curfew.

An autopsy on the bodies showed they had multiple injuries on the head and elbows.

Some local leaders and organisations have petitioned the IG to institute an independent probe into the incident.
