Uhuru, Ruto Should Reason Together to End Tension in the Country, Martha Karua

Publish date: 2024-04-14
  • Martha Karua urged President Uhuru Kenyatta and his Deputy William Ruto to end apparent political differences
  • The NARC Kenya leader said the frosty relationship between the two leaders was bad for the country
  • Uhuru for the first time on August 23, dared Ruto to resign from the Jubilee government as he is seemingly dissatisfied with it

NARC Kenya party leader Martha Karua has called on President Uhuru Kenyatta and his Deputy William Ruto to end apparent political differences between them.

Karua urged the two leaders to reason together to end political tension among their respective supporters adding that the frosty relationship was not good for the country.

"I'm calling upon the president and his deputy to reason together so as to reduce the tension which is building across the country," she said during a function at a Kerugoya church on Saturday, August 28.

At the same time, Karua who was accompanied by the National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi and Kirinyaga county deputy governor Peter Ndambiri declared her intention to challenge Governor Anne Waiguru in 2022.

“I’m ready to give my competitors a run for their money. I want to be the next Kirinyaga governor so that I can serve my people who are currently suffering,” she told the gathering.

The two women went head to head in the 2017 General Election in which Waiguru, running on a Jubilee Party ticket floored Karua to be declared the first county female governor.

Resign if unhappy

Uhuru for the first time on Monday, August 23, dared Ruto to resign from the Jubilee government as he is seemingly dissatisfied with it.

The head of state Uhuru dropped the bombshell on his deputy of nine years further widening the rift between the two top leaders who started reading from different political scripts soon after their re-election in 2017.

In an interview with media editors at State House Nairobi, a seemingly vexed Uhuru said if Ruto was as unhappy as he seemed with his government, he should resign and take his agenda to the people.

"It would be an honourable thing that if you are not happy with it (government), step aside and allow then take your agenda to the people. That is what happens in a democracy, you cannot ride on what we have done and talk a different language on the side," he said.
