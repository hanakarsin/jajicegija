I have fresh evidence about Garissa governor Ali Korane in KSh 233m graft case, DPP tells court

Publish date: 2024-06-15

- Director of Public Prosecutions Noordin Haji revealed the fresh information as the case came for hearing before anti-corruption magistrate Douglas Ogoti

- The state sought 14 days to serve Korane’s defence with the new evidence, adding more time for the team representing the accused persons to prepare

- The governor has been barred from accessing office and was ordered to deposit his passport with the court

The Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) Noordin Haji has revealed that he has new evidence against Garissa governor Ali Korane in a graft case over the misappropriation of KSh 233 million meant for a World Bank-funded project.

On Monday, March 1, the case came for hearing before anti-corruption magistrate Douglas Ogoti.

During the session, the DPP’s counsel Mercy Gatur said that they have new information about the graft case against Korane and his co-accused persons.

She told the court that in the course of pretrial with the witnesses, they discovered new evidence which will be vital for the case.

As such, the state sought 14 days to serve Korane’s defence with the new evidence, a request Ogoti granted, adding a further two weeks for the team representing the accused persons to prepare.

The case was adjourned until April 19 for the hearing.

Korane was charged in September 2020 alongside the county finance chief officer Ibrahim Nur Malow, head of treasury Mohammed Ahmed Abdullahi, municipality county executive officer Abdi Shale and the municipal head of accounting Ahmed Abdullahi.

They denied the eight criminal counts.

The governor has been barred from accessing office and was ordered to deposit his passport with the court.

The charges involve a conspiracy to commit an offence of economic crime, misappropriation of public funds, and willful failure to comply with the law relating to the management of funds.

They are accused of committing offences between February 25, and September 30, 2019.

The governor faced a separate count involving willful failure to comply with laws relating to the management of funds and the use of county resources.

The court heard that the governor’s action resulted to the mismanagement of KSh 233.5 million allocated to the county government as a conditional grant for the Kenya Urban Support Program (KUSP).

According to the charge sheet, the five accused misappropriated a conditional grant of Sh233.5 million that had been allocated to the county government under the KUSP.

Among the governors who have so far been prohibited from office is Samburu’s Moses Lenolkulal, Mike Sonko (Nairobi), Okoth Obado (Migori), Muthomi Njuki (Tharaka-Nithi) and Kiambu’s former governor Ferdinand Waititu.
