James Gikara: Autopsy Shows Nakuru Doctor Died from Ingesting Medicinal Substance

Publish date: 2024-06-06
  • James Gakara had swallowed a foreign substance that could have led to his demise on the morning of Wednesday, September 22
  • The postmortem did not establish whether or not the medicine was administered by the deceased
  • Government pathologist Ngulungu conducted the exercise at the Nakuru Level Five Hospital mortuary

An autopsy conducted on the body of the late Nakuru gynaecologist has been released.

The postmortem revealed James Gakara had swallowed a foreign substance that could have led to his demise on the morning of Wednesday, September 22.

Government pathologist, Titus Ngulungu, who conducted the exercise at the Nakuru Level Five Hospital mortuary on Friday, September 24, highly suspected the substance might be medicine.

"As a result of my examination, I have formed the opinion that the cause of death was a foreign substance suspected to be medicine,” he stated.

Ngulungu was, however, quick to restate that he would make a conclusive report upon further analysis of body tissue samples at the Government Chemist.

“For now we cannot establish if the medicine in his body was administered by him or by a doctor. We have taken organ and tissue samples to the Government Chemist, where they will be analysed to establish if there was something else and if it was poisonous,” he added.

The postmortem did not establish whether or not the medicine was administered by the deceased and this will form the basis of the homicide team probing the matter.

Family turned away

Earlier in the day, the family had been turned away by the morgue after they failed to prove Gakara was their kin since he was booked in as an unknown male adult.

Family spokesperson Burton Njoroge told journalists that they had been directed to file a next of kin affidavit before they could be allowed to access the body.

"We came here in the morning expecting that the postmortem will be conducted but we were sent away to bring the documents," Njoroge stated.

Two children dead

Gakara was found lying unconscious inside his apartment on the night of Saturday, September 18. Next to him were the bodies of his two children Dylan, 5, and Karuana, 3.

His wife, Winni Odhiambo was in Nairobi when the incident happened and was informed of her children's death later.

Inside the house located at Kiamunyi estate, police found several vials of drugs and syringes in a room where the bodies lay.

Detectives suspected Gakara injected the minors with the drugs before attempting to take his own life.
