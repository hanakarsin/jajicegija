Stop blackmailing Uhuru into supporting you - ODM MP tells Ruto

Publish date: 0001-01-01

- The MP blamed Senator Murkomen for leading the 2022 debate

- He urged Ruto to show leadership in his camp by prevailing on his lieutenants

- He asked leaders to help Uhuru build a better legacy

An Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party lawmaker has lashed out at Deputy President William Ruto and his close allies for 'nagging' President Uhuru Kenyatta at the expense of development.

Kisumu Central MP Fred Ouda claimed Ruto's camp was blackmailing the Head of State into supporting his 2022 presidential ambition, saying such action was detrimental to service delivery.

"I don't think it is right and correct to issue ultimatums and threats to the President of Kenya on the matters 2022 election. It is just the other day Uhuru and Raila met to heal this country and right now the main agenda is to support the handshake," he said.

The former MCA blamed Elgeyo Marakwet Senator Kipchumba Murkomen for leading the 2022 debate.

He urged Ruto to show leadership in his camp by prevailing on his lieutenants to stop the Uhuru succession debate since the country was still reeling from effect of post 2017 election violence which sharply left Kenya divided.

"We must support Uhuru so that he can leave behind a better legacy. So we are not interested about succession politics. It is not about Ruto. Who said it has to be Ruto? We have many people who can become presidents of Kenya," the first timer legislator added.

With the General Election four years away, the political atmosphere appears already polarised as leaders from both divide out-rightly engaging in succession politics.

Even though Ruto and Uhuru signed a pre-election pact prior to the 2013 presidential election, opinion is still divided on whether the the Head of State will honour it.

Ruto's camp formerly made of members of his defunct United Republican Party (URP) has been leading the debate.
