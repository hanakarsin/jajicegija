Peter Opondo: Media Boss Opens Up On Short Stint at Standard Group, Says He Was Fired

Publish date: 2024-08-10
  • Peter Opondo said he had never been sacked since he had been doing his work diligently
  • Opondo divulged that in his 20 years of working experience, he has never been sacked for "unsatisfactory performance"
  • The media practitioner stated that he didn't receive any formal appraisal or feedback and there was no documentation about the alleged poor performance

Standard Group's Media Strategy and Content Editor Peter Opondo has been fired.

First-time to be fired

According to Opondo, it was his first time to be fired, saying that in his 20 years of working experience, he has never been dismissed for "unsatisfactory performance".

"This past week, the Standard Group PLC sacked me. I have worked for many employers over the past 20 years, it is the first time I am terminated for 'unsatisfactory performance'," he said.

The media practitioner claimed that he didn't receive any formal appraisal or feedback and there was no documentation about the alleged poor performance.

"There was never a discussion with or feedback from my boss, there was no PIP. Just a sacking letter. The HR honcho just told me my boss didn't think I was the right person for the job," he noted.

Opondo went on to say that the company asked him to resign voluntarily to "protect his professional reputation" or face the sack.

"I chose to embrace my sacking. I was fired. It is what it is. This is nothing to be ashamed about...it's someone's opinion about me, it doesn't change who I am. I am pretty sure I will be vindicated soon," Opondo said.

Before joining KTN, Opondo worked for K24. He has also held various roles at Citizen TV including interim Editorial Director. He has also worked at NTV Uganda.

Joshua Makori Ongechi fired

In another news TUKO.co.ke reported that journalist Joshua Makori Ongechi expressed his disappointment following the termination of his contract at Citizen TV just when his star was beginning to shine.

Ongechi who was a renowned education reporter left Citizen TV on Friday, March 12, having worked in the media house for four years.

Makori said the surprise dismissal from Royal Media Services which he had called home for over three years, cut short his dreams and aspirations of his parents who were always praying for him.

The reporter believed all that he needed was guidance from his senior colleagues to grow into a professional journalist of international repute.
