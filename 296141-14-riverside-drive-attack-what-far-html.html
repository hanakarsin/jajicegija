14 Riverside Drive attack: What we know so far

Publish date: 0001-01-01

Kenyans are still trying to come to terms with the suspected terror attack which took place on the afternoon of Tuesday, January 15, at the luxurious DusitD2 hotel, Riverside Drive in Nairobi.

At least three cars have been reduced to ashes while scores have been injured following the attack.

Even as details of the attack continue to unfold, here is what TUKO.co.ke has gathered from the time the attack commenced:

3.48pm: Several suspected terrorists arrive at the DusitD2 Hotel on Riverside Drive in a silver Toyota Ractis KCN 340E and proceed to walk to the security guards at the gate. They throw an explosive device towards the guards.

3.50pm: Witnesses hear a loud bang resembling a grenade explosion in Riverside area, with a large cloud of smoke seen soon after.

3.55pm: Three vehicles go up in flames

3.58pm: Director of Criminal Investigations George Kinoti arrives at the scene

4.pm: Riverside is officially a trending topic as social media users go into a frenzy following reports it could possibly be a terrorist attack

4.30pm: Several people confirmed dead and scored injured

Terror group al-Shabaab has since claimed responsibility for the attack through a statement to al-Jazeera.

This attack comes exactly two years after the al-Shabaab insurgents laid ambush on Kenya Defence Forces camp in El Adde Somalia, on Friday, January 15, 2016.

5pm: It is confirmed the injured were being rushed to Kenyatta National Hospital, M.P Hospital, Aga Khan Hospital and Avenue with an urgent blood donation appeal made.

6:00 pm: Inspector General of Police Joseph Boinnet addresses a press conference confirming police suspect the attack to be an act of terror asks Kenyans to stay calm.

6.01pm: State House warns against sharing clips and photos of the attacks on social media.

6.20pm: US elite forces join the efforts to quell the situation which is still being considered active
