Safaricom cuts data prices in the wake of increased competition

Publish date: 2024-02-02

- New charges were announced by South Africa-based Vodacom Group, a major shareholder in Safaricom

- Vodacom said decision to lower data prices was informed by a mix of regulatory and competitive pressures

- Kenya had the largest price cut, followed by DRC with 29.2%, Lesotho 28.9%, South Africa 23.3% and Tanzania 13.8% inVodacom's latest price review

Telecommunications company Safaricom has reduced prices of its data bundles by 42% amid fierce competition from other data service providers.

The telco which offers a wide range of internet packages slashed the charges per Megabyte (MB) and is expected to lower the prices further in the near future in a move to drive data inclusion and also stay ahead of rivals.

The new charges announced by South Africa-based Vodacom Group Limited, which owns 35% shares in Safaricom, will apply to the Group's business associates and subsidiaries operating in different markets across Africa.

“In the context of our commitment to drive digital inclusion, and given the tough consumer environment, we recognise the imperative to further lower data prices in some of our markets.We will ensure this does not compromise our ability to make the investments in network and IT infrastructure needed to broaden service delivery and maintain high quality offerings,” Vodacom said as quoted by Daily Nation on Tuesday, July 9.

According to the telecom giant, the decision to lower the data prices was informed by a mix of regulatory and competitive pressures.

Compared to the other African markets, Kenya had the largest price cut, followed by Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) with 29.2%, Lesotho 28.9%, South Africa 23.3% and Tanzania 13.8%.

Safaricom has been facing cutthroat competition from Airtel Kenya and Telkom Kenya in the local data market amid rising demand for data and internet services in the country.

As of December 2017, internet access in Kenya had hit 89.4% going by the International Telecommunications Union (ITU) report.
