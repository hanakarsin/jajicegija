Sharon Otieno: Court Rejects Governor Obado's Plea to Postpone Case, Sets Hearing on July 12

Publish date: 2024-07-15
  • Migori Governor Okoth Obado's plea to adjourn the hearing of murdered university student Sharon Otieno's case was denied
  • The hearing was slated for July 12 and the prosecution was directed to prepare its witnesses for the trial
  • Sharon's lifeless body was on the evening of Tuesday, September 4, 2018, discovered in a thicket in Oyugis
  • Her mother confirmed her daughter had an affair with the Migori county boss in the months leading to her death

Migori governor Zachary Okoth Obado has been dealt a blow after the court rejected his plea to postpone the murder case of Rongo University student Sharon Otieno, of which the county chief is the main suspect.

The controversial governor, a prime suspect in the gruesome murder of Sharon and her unborn child, had sought to have the case postponed citing COVID-19 restrictions which he said would interfere with his travel to Nairobi for the hearing.

In the ruling delivered by Justice Cecelia Githua at the Milimani Law Courts on Monday, July 5, Obado's application was denied and the hearing date was set for July 12.

The prosecution was also directed to present its witnesses. The prosecution has lined up 37 witnesses to testify against the county boss and his two co-accused persons Michael Juma Oyamo and Casper Obiero. Two of the witnesses are under protection.

As earlier reported, the governor, in a letter to the Director of Public Prosecution, had cited COVID-19 restrictions, which barred him from travelling to Nairobi for the trial.

His lawyer Kioko Kilukumi said since Migori was already a COVID-19 hotspot, it would be prudent if a virtual court case was held and new dates set.

However, during the court hearing on Monday, the deceased's family lawyer Daniel Njoroge and the prosecution vehemently opposed the adjournment saying the matter has not commenced nearly 3 years after the murder of Sharon and her unborn child.

Sharon's death

Sharon's lifeless body was on the evening of Tuesday, September 4, 2018, discovered in a thicket in Oyugis and her death elicited a heated public uproar.

She seven months pregnant at the time of her brutal death and it was established the county official was responsible.

Her mother confirmed her daughter had an affair with the county boss in the months leading to her death.

The governor is, however, on record denying any association with her.
