Mike Sonko Talks About Marrying Early, Tells Men to Respect Wives

Publish date: 2024-06-29

- Former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko has surfaced online with a message to couples whose marriages seem not to be working

- His message comes at a time when cases of domestic violence are on the rise

- Sonko is married to one Primrose Mbuvi, and they are blessed with five children: three daughters and two adopted sons

Former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko has been lying low like an envelope since he was impeached in December 2020.

The flamboyant politician shared his two cents on domestic violence. Cases of domestic violence have been rampant of late, and two women were killed by their lovers in the past week.

Sonko advised his followers to marry early, urging them to respect their parents and wives.

"I thought I was the only one who got married and started a family at an early age...kumbe tuko wengi (we are many of us)," he posted on Instagram.

Using an example of a recent domestic violence case, Sonko advised couples to solve their problems amicably without harming or killing one another.

"The recent cases of domestic violence involving the killing of spouses, children and even parents like the case of the young man in Kisumu are extremely disturbing, and we need to bring this bad culture to an end as Kenyans. The only secret for survival of any marriage or relationship, when your wife ako juu wewe kuwa chini and vice versa. Ukikosa Sema pole and vice versa. Hasira hasara. (When your wife is angry, don't overeact. Say sorry and move on because anger can lead you to make bad decisions)," he said.

His advice comes barely a week after TUKO.co.ke published the story of Evans Karani, a man who confessed to killing his secret lover Catherine Nyokabi in Kiambu county.

Karani badly disfigured her body, and while he was trying to dispose of her remains, his vehicle got stuck in the middle of the muddy Bob Harris Road in Juja, forcing him to flee.

Nyokabi's body was discovered by residents of the area who reported the incident to the police.
