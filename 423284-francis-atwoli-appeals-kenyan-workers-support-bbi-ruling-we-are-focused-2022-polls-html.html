Francis Atwoli Appeals to Kenyan Workers to Support BBI Ruling: 'We Are Focused on 2022 Polls

Publish date: 2024-05-29
  • The Central Organization of Trade Union Secretary (COTU) boss is one of the proponents of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) Bill
  • A seven-judge Court of Appeal bench sitting in Nairobi on Friday, August 20, found the BBI process unconstitutional
  • The voluminous Appellate Court judgment presented far-reaching political and legal implications

Central Organization of Trade Union Secretary (COTU) secretary-general Francis Atwoli has appealed to Kenyan workers to support the Appellate Court ruling on the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) Bill delivered on Friday, August 20.

In a statement seen by TUKO.co.ke, Atwoli said that COTU prides itself in being the proponent of the BBI.

''During the 2017 International Labour Day, I asked Kenyans to rethink amending the constitution sp as to achieve political stability. I was, without doubt, the first leader that came out publicly and called for a constitutional change,'' Atwoli said.

COTU is passionate about politics

Atwoli said that trade unionists are passionate about politics because politics determines most things in any given country.

''It is the politics that determine who gets what, when and how. Therefore, the role of politics in the country's development and especially the workers cannot be downplayed,'' Atwoli explained.

He said that the BBI was close to his heart because of his love for the country.

''I, therefore, would appeal particularly to workers that I represent, more than 4 million of them, to support the ruling. We don't want to go against it; we are not appealing; we will not push politicians to make sure they go to court and appeal,'' he said.

The veteran trade unionist termed 2022 as a year of change to the needs of workers.

''I want to make it clear that the BBI ruling is behind us now, and as the workers, we are the people who were hard hit. But I must also say that there will be many changes after the forthcoming polls favourable to the amendments of the Kenyan constitution,'' said the unionist.

Focusing on the 2022 General Election

He said that COTU is focusing on the 2022 General Election, maintaining that they can see victory at the end of the tunnel.

''Those celebrating can continue doing so, but their celebrations might be cut short by the results that they might not be able to accept or agree with in the next year's polls,'' he said.

A seven-judge Court of Appeal bench on Friday, August 20, found the process unconstitutional and handed the promoters a blow that could potentially stop the sounds and drumbeats of BBI reggae.

The voluminous judgment presented far-reaching political and legal implications and exposed President Uhuru Kenyatta and his role in the amendment of the 2010 Constitution.

The Court of Appeal president Daniel Musinga and judge Patrick Kiage, in their lengthy submissions, agreed with the bench that the president's role is limited to protecting and defending the Constitution.
