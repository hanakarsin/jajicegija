According to a new Forbes article published on Monday, Shawn Corey Carter (His real name) has so far amassed a fortune that together totals to 1 billion dollars. This makes him one of the handfuls of singers in the world who are billionaires.

The rapper was first featured on the 2010’s Forbes cover of the 400 richest Americans. His wealth has since been estimated by the financial outlet. He has over the years, made huge stakes in D’Usse cognac and Armand de Brignac, which are $100 and $310 million worth respectively.

5. Rihanna makes it to the top of the richest women in the world.

Popular songstress, Robyn Fenty Rihanna has evolved to become the first non-white American to own a luxurious fashion store.

Rihanna is a makeup and style icon entrepreneur whose efforts add up to a 600 million dollars fortune. This makes her among the richest woman ahead of Beyonce, Madonna, and Celine Dion.

As we all know, Rihanna is a singer. However, most of her income doesn’t come from singing. She has partnered with LVMH, a French luxury good company run by Bernard Arnault. The company has also greatly contributed to her make-up brand, “Fenty Beauty,” which is becoming a viral success quickly. It is reported that in the first few weeks, the brand made 100 million dollar sales that were hugely propelled by Rihanna’s 71 million followers on Instagram.

The rappers empire is growing day by day. In May this year, Forbes reports that together with LVMH, they announced a new clothing shop that will manufacture classic high-end shoes, accessories, clothes, and jewelry.

They extended the offer to me, and it was a no-brainer because LVMH is a machine. Bernard Arnault was so enthusiastic. He trusted me and my vision, Rihanna told The New York Times.

The fashion line which opened online on May will exist under the usual famous brands such as LVMHI, Dior, and Givenchy.

8. Ian Wainaina pays tribute to Afro-jazz musician Lydia Achieng’ Abura

Award-winning choreographer and dancer Ian Wainaina has now paid tribute to Afro-Jazz musician Lydia Achieng’ Abura.

Abura died in 2016, and she was a real influencer in the Kenyan music industry. She left a couple of albums and many artists who she natured by herself.

Ian recalls working with the songstress in Tusker Project Fame (TPF) where she was acting as the academy’s principal.

I remember she hated going upstairs, but she loved all the contestant, and she would encourage us so much. She would take us for lunch and would share a lot of stories with us. Like I remember, she told us she started driving a car at the age of 12, Ian told The Star newspaper.

Ian Wainaina has been absent from the music industry for almost ten years. However, he has broken the silence with his new song titled, “Mrembo.” The song, just as the name suggests, focuses on a certain beautiful girl whom Ian values so much.

When asked why he decided to come back to the music industry, Eric revealed that it was his young dancers that encouraged him.

I was challenged by two of my youngest dancers to go back to the studio after nudging them always to follow their dreams, he said.
