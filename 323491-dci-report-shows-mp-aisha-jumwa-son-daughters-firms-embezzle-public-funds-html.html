DCI report shows MP Aisha Jumwa used son, daughter's firms to embezzle public funds

Publish date: 2024-08-21

- Jumwa was accused of squandering public funds to the tune of KSh 500,000

- The vocal Malindi MP allegedly used firms owned by her son and daughter to defraud the public

- According to sleuths, Kaseru construction company owned by Jumwa's children was used to receive and transfer suspicious payments

- Another company under probe is Gadu Stores which is solely owned by Jumwa’s son, Karisa Kalume

- The legislator, her family and close associates are said to have received close to KSh 100 million of the total amount under probe

A report from the Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) has revealed Malindi MP Aisha Jumwa allegedly used firms owned by her two children to embezzle public funds.

The embattled lawmaker who is under investigation over suspicious transfer of KSh 500 million from Constituency Development Funds (CDF) kitty is said to have colluded with his son and daughter to complete the corruption circuit.

Jumwa who appeared before the DCI on Thursday, November 7, was tasked to explain the suspicious transactions that involved four bank accounts linked to her, her two children and other accomplices.

It was alleged Karisa Kalume and Selina Selume Sidi who are son and daughter of Jumwa and directors of Kaseru construction company, which was registered on January 15, 2014, received KSh.64,735, 504 from Kilifi county between March and August 2018, Citizen Digital reported.

The report further indicated that after the payment was deposited, the Malindi lawmaker coincidentally received KSh 3 million as payment for services rendered to Kilifi county government through several tenders.

Likoni MP Mishi Juma was also mentioned as a beneficiary of the loot with reports indicating she received KSh 3 million.

The same account operated by Jumwa's children allegedly transferred KSh 5 million to Likoni MP Mishi Mboko and another KSh 15 million to Jumwa.

Another company under probe was Gadu Stores which is also owned by Jumwa’s son who is said to be a sole proprietor of the firm which was registered on April 11, 2014.

According to the DCI, the legislator, her family members and close associates received close to KSh 100 million of the total amount under probe.

TUKO.co.ke understands the funds were paid as kickbacks from projects undertaken by the county government and the constituency development kitty.

Chief sleuth George Kinoti sent detectives to Malindi to investigate the area MP after the money in question was traced to her, her children and aides' accounts.

The DCI is seeking to have Jumwa and 10 others prosecuted for squandering public funds, abuse of office, failing to adhere to procurement procedures and conspiracy to defraud among others.
